# Triple Brain Marketing Blog Post -- Design Spec

## Overview

A ~4,500-word MDX blog post positioning Rob Palmer's "Triple Brain Marketing" as a unique, unmatched approach to copywriting and marketing. The post follows a classic DR structure: lead with pain (three failed approaches), reveal the solution (Triple Brain), bridge to results, close hard on booking a call.

## Target Audience

Business owners and marketers shopping for a copywriter who need to understand why Rob is different from other options (traditional copywriters, DIY AI, "AI-powered" marketers).

## Primary CTA

Book a call via Calendly.

## Tone & Positioning

- Classic DR structure: problem > agitate > solution > proof > close
- "You're outgunned" emotional core
- Confident, authoritative, specific over generic
- Not educational/teaching -- this is a "why hire me" post with enough substance to earn the pitch

## Post Structure

### Opening Hook

Uncomfortable truth: right now, your competitors are making one of three marketing mistakes, and every one is costing them money. Tease: there's a fourth option nobody else can offer. Establish "you're outgunned" tension immediately.

### KeyTakeaways Component

4-5 bullets previewing:
- What Triple Brain Marketing is
- Why single-brain approaches fail (all three of them)
- What the three brains are (experience, AI skills, vault)
- Why this changes results for the reader's business

### Section 1: "Failure #1: The Traditional Copywriter" (H2)

- Good copywriter, one brain, working alone
- Can't process competitive landscape in seconds, can't run 50 headline variations against proven frameworks, can't cross-reference what's working in the market right now
- "Bringing a knife to a drone fight"
- What's missing: AI leverage

### Section 2: "Failure #2: The DIY AI Approach" (H2)

- Fired the copywriter, went to ChatGPT
- Fast output, flat results -- generic, no awareness levels, no mechanism, no emotional architecture
- "AI without direct response expertise produces content, not copy"
- "Reads like a term paper pretending to be a sales letter"
- What's missing: 40 years of knowing what actually converts

### Section 3: "Failure #3: The 'AI-Powered' Marketer" (H2)

- Found a marketer who claims to use AI -- better, but...
- Prompting a general-purpose tool with generic instructions
- No curated vault of proven swipe from the greats
- No 512 pieces of battle-tested copy to draw from
- No detailed notes from RMBC II, Great Leads, Breakthrough Advertising
- "Flying a fighter jet with a learner's permit"
- What's missing: the vault

### DefinitionBox Component

After the three failures, formally define "Triple Brain Marketing":
> The integration of human expertise, AI capability, and a proprietary knowledge system to produce copy that no single approach can match.

### Section 4: "Triple Brain Marketing" -- The Reveal (H2)

Transitional paragraph: "There's a fourth option. I call it Triple Brain Marketing. And I'm the only person in the world who can deliver it."

#### Brain #1: 40 Years of Direct Response Expertise (H3)

- $523M+ in tracked results
- Campaigns across health, biz op, finance, survival
- Schwartz awareness levels, Masterson lead types, Georgi mechanisms
- Pattern recognition built over four decades of copy that had to sell or the client didn't pay

#### Brain #2: Claude Code + Custom Copywriting Skills (H3)

- Not generic AI -- Claude Code running purpose-built DR skills
- Structured skill files encoding specific frameworks, formulas, quality standards
- Skills for: sales copy, ad copy, landing pages, copychiefing, compliance checking
- Link to skills post as proof: `/blog/claude-code-copywriting-skills`
- Brief mention: "I built these skills. They're free. You can see exactly how they work."

#### Brain #3: The Copywriting Vault (H3)

- Proprietary Obsidian knowledge base
- 190 proven frameworks and principles
- 193 swipe pieces from the greatest copywriters who ever lived
- 125 sets of detailed course and book notes (Breakthrough Advertising, Boron Letters, RMBC II, $100M Offers)
- 76 battle-ready templates
- 512 pieces of own copy -- tested, measured, refined
- Cross-referenced by copywriter, by market, by persuasion technique
- "When I write copy, Claude doesn't just have AI -- it has my library"

#### ExpertQuote Component

A relevant quote from Halbert, Schwartz, or another DR great about studying proven winners / standing on the shoulders of giants.

### Section 5: "Why It Matters To You" -- The Bridge (H2)

Short section -- 2-3 paragraphs max. Key argument:

- Each brain multiplies the others (not additive -- compound)
- Experience tells Claude WHAT to write
- Skills tell Claude HOW to write it
- Vault gives Claude proven material to draw from
- Result: weeks become days, 60% accuracy becomes 95%, six revisions become one or two

#### ComparisonTable Component

| Capability | Traditional Copywriter | DIY AI | "AI-Powered" Marketer | Triple Brain |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| DR expertise | Yes | No | Sometimes | 40 years |
| AI speed | No | Yes | Yes | Yes + custom skills |
| Proven swipe/frameworks | Maybe their own | No | No | 1,239-file vault |
| Result | Good but slow | Fast but flat | Fast but shallow | Fast, deep, and proven |

### Section 6: The Close (H2)

- Hard CTA, no ambiguity
- "If you want Triple Brain Marketing working on your business, there's one way to get it."
- Calendly link (book a call)
- Brief urgency reinforcement: competitors are choosing between three losing options
- No soft "learn more" or "explore services" -- straight to the call
