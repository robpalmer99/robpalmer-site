## What Is a VSL?

If you are new to the format, start here. If you already know, skip to the next section.

<DefinitionBox term="VSL (Video Sales Letter)">
A persuasive video presentation built on a direct-response copywriting script, engineered to guide a viewer through a complete persuasion sequence — from hook through mechanism, proof, and close — with the goal of producing an immediate, measurable conversion. The format typically runs 10-45 minutes and remains the highest-ROI format in direct response marketing, with average conversion rates rising to 1.78% across the Vidalytics platform. For a deep dive on the format itself, see my complete guide on [what a VSL is and how it works](/blog/what-is-a-vsl).
</DefinitionBox>

Jon Benson invented the VSL in 2007. Ugly text on screen, read aloud. Terrible production quality. It crushed everything else — and kept crushing. The format has generated over $1 billion in tracked revenue because it does one thing no other format can: the copywriter controls the pacing. The viewer cannot skip ahead to the price. They experience the persuasion sequence in exactly the order you intended.

That advantage is why [VSL marketing continues to accelerate](/blog/state-of-vsl-marketing-2026), not decline. And this guide shows you how to exploit it.
