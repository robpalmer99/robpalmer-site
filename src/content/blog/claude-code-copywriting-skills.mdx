## The Five Skills

### 1. [Direct Response Copy](/blog/claude-code-direct-response-copy-skill) — The Writing Foundation

This skill writes sales pages, VSLs, emails, headlines, CTAs, and any persuasive copy. It contains the complete frameworks from Schwartz, Hopkins, Ogilvy, Halbert, Caples, Sugarman, and Collier — not name-dropped, but applied. Five proven headline formulas. Six opening techniques. The So What? Chain for drilling past "saves time" to the emotional payoff that actually closes.

What makes it different from generic AI: every feature gets pushed through the So What? Chain three levels deep. A "fast database" becomes "you stop waking up stressed about churn." That is the level of specificity this skill enforces.

### 2. [Ad Copy](/blog/claude-code-ad-copy-skill) — Meta Ads That Stop the Scroll

This skill writes high-converting Meta (Facebook + Instagram) ad copy — primary text, headlines, descriptions, and UGC/video ad scripts. It includes eight proven hook types, the WHY/WHAT/HOW structure from Alen Sultanic, three video script formats (short UGC, ADHD super hook, story-style), and the critical strategic decision most AI skips entirely: whether to sell the click or sell the solution.

What makes it different: it understands that Meta ads live or die in the first 125 characters before the "See more" break. Every ad it writes treats those characters as a headline — not a preamble.

### 3. [Landing Page Copy](/blog/claude-code-landing-page-copy-skill) — Bridge Pages That Drive Clicks

This skill writes short-form bridge pages and pre-sell pages — the 300-800 word pages that sit between ads and VSLs. It uses the Three-Paragraph Framework (Discovery/Authority, Social Proof/Results, CTA/Urgency) that winning landing pages follow, plus the mechanism tease, the "Nothing To Do With" elimination pattern, and visceral language that makes the problem felt rather than merely understood.

What makes it different: it knows a bridge page is NOT a sales page. It does not mention products, prices, or offers. It builds curiosity, establishes authority, and makes the CTA the only logical next step — which is exactly what a bridge page needs to do.

### 4. [Copychief](/blog/claude-code-copychief-skill) — Expert Copy Review in Seconds

This skill turns Claude Code into a structured copy reviewer — the kind of senior creative director (or senior [creative strategist](/blog/creative-strategist)) at a top direct response agency. It evaluates strategic architecture first (big idea, mechanism, offer stack, emotional throughline) before touching individual lines, then provides section-by-section feedback with specific rewrites and the direct response principle behind each suggestion.

What makes it different: every issue gets four elements — Location, Issue, Fix, Principle. Not "make the headline stronger" but the exact line, what is wrong, a drop-in rewrite, and *why* the change matters. That is the difference between a horoscope and a copy review.

### 5. [Compliance Checker](/blog/claude-code-compliance-checker-skill) — Stop Getting Banned

This skill checks ads, landing pages, VSLs, and emails against platform compliance rules for Meta, Google, TikTok, YouTube, and ClickBank. It runs a 9-point checklist audit, then goes line-by-line flagging issues by severity (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) with compliant rewrites that preserve persuasive power. Includes a trigger words reference file with compliant substitutions for every common violation.

What makes it different: it knows the difference between what gets flagged on Meta versus YouTube. It knows "regulate blood sugar" is a definitive claim but "helps support blood sugar" is compliant. It knows digital products face lighter scrutiny than supplements. This is not generic caution — it is platform-specific compliance knowledge.
