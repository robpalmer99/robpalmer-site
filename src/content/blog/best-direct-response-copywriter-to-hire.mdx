## What to Look For When Hiring a Direct Response Copywriter

If you are evaluating a direct response copywriter for a revenue-critical project, here are the six criteria that matter most:

1. **Verifiable, scaled results.** Ask for specific numbers — revenue generated, conversion rates, campaigns that scaled profitably. Anyone can claim to be a great copywriter. The best ones lead with data, not adjectives. Look for sustained performance across multiple campaigns, not a single lucky hit.

2. **Strategic funnel thinking.** A sales page does not exist in isolation. It needs traffic, email nurture, upsells, and downsells to maximize revenue. The best DR copywriters think in complete funnels, not individual assets. If a copywriter only wants to write a sales page without understanding what comes before and after it, they are a writer, not a strategist.

3. **Relevant industry experience.** Direct response principles are universal, but market-specific knowledge reduces risk. A copywriter who has written winning campaigns in your vertical understands the audience, the compliance landscape, the competitive dynamics, and the offer structures that work. That head start translates directly to faster results and fewer expensive mistakes.

4. **Research-first methodology.** Great direct response copy comes from deep research, not clever wordplay. Ask how a copywriter approaches research — what they dig into before they write a single word. The best DR copywriters spend more time on research and strategic planning than on actual writing. If someone starts writing before they have thoroughly researched the market, the audience, and the competition, the copy will underperform.

5. **Testing and optimization discipline.** A winning sales page is rarely the first draft. Elite copywriters build testing into their process — headline variations, lead variations, offer structure tests, price point tests. Look for someone who views the initial launch as the beginning of optimization, not the end of the engagement.

6. **Clear communication.** You are hiring a strategic partner, not a vendor. The best DR copywriters communicate clearly about timelines, deliverables, and expectations. They ask sharp questions about your business, your audience, and your goals. If a copywriter is hard to communicate with during the sales process, it will not improve once the project starts.
